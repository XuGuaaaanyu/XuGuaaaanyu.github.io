This note covers some of the fundamental concepts in Lie group and Lie algebra, and their applications to representing rigid body motion in robotics.

Matrix Lie Groups

A group is a set GGG﻿ equipped with a binary map ⋅:G×G→G\cdot:G\times G\to G⋅:G×G→G﻿ satisfying the following properties:

  • Closure: ∀a,b∈G,a⋅b∈G\forall a,b\in G, a\cdot b\in G∀a,b∈G,a⋅b∈G﻿.
  • Associativity: ∀a,b,c∈G, (a⋅b)⋅c=a⋅(b⋅c)\forall a, b, c\in G,\, (a\cdot b)\cdot c = a\cdot(b\cdot c)∀a,b,c∈G,(a⋅b)⋅c=a⋅(b⋅c)﻿.
  • Identity: ∃e∈G, s.t. ∀a∈G, a⋅e=e⋅a=a\exist e\in G,\,\mathrm{s.t.}\,\forall a\in G, \, a\cdot e=e\cdot a=a∃e∈G,s.t.∀a∈G,a⋅e=e⋅a=a﻿.
  • Inverse: ∀a∈G, ∃a−1∈G, s.t. a⋅a−1=a−1⋅a=e\forall a\in G,\, \exist a^{-1}\in G, \, \mathrm{s.t.}\, a\cdot a^{-1}=a^{-1}\cdot a = e∀a∈G,∃a−1∈G,s.t.a⋅a−1=a−1⋅a=e﻿.

Here, it’s better to understand the element of a group as a kind of operation or transformation (e.g., a rotation or a rigid body transformation) rather than a number or a vector. These groups turn out to be also differentiable manifolds, which makes them Lie groups.

Lie Algebra

A Lie algebra is a vector space g\mathfrak{g}g﻿ together with a binary operation called the Lie bracket [⋅,⋅]:g×g→g[\cdot,\cdot]:\mathfrak{g}\times\mathfrak{g}\to\mathfrak{g}[⋅,⋅]:g×g→g﻿ satisfying the following properties

  • Bilinearity: [ax+by,z]=a[x,z]+b[y,z][a\mathbf{x}+b\mathbf{y},\mathbf{z}]=a[\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}]+b[\mathbf{y},z][ax+by,z]=a[x,z]+b[y,z]﻿, [z,ax+by]=a[z,x]+b[z,y][\mathbf{z},a\mathbf{x}+b\mathbf{y}]=a[\mathbf{z},\mathbf{x}]+b[\mathbf{z},\mathbf{y}][z,ax+by]=a[z,x]+b[z,y]﻿.
  • Alternating: [x,x]=0[\mathbf{x},\mathbf{x}]=\mathbf{0}[x,x]=0﻿.
  • Jacobi identity: [x,[y,z]]+[y,[z,x]]+[z,[x,y]]=0[\mathbf{x},[\mathbf{y},\mathbf{z}]]+[\mathbf{y},[\mathbf{z},\mathbf{x}]]+[\mathbf{z},[\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}]]=\mathbf{0}[x,[y,z]]+[y,[z,x]]+[z,[x,y]]=0﻿.

Importantly, every Lie algebra g\mathfrak{g}g﻿ is associated to a Lie group GGG﻿, which is the tangent space of the identity of the Lie group, denoted as g=TeG\mathfrak{g} = T_{e}Gg=Te​G﻿, and it completely captures the local structure of the group. In the case of robot motion, we can think of a trajectory that lives in the Lie group while the velocity lives in the Lie algebra.
